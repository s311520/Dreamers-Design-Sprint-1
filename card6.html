What to avoid in formal writing

When writing an essay, it is important to construct a reasoned argument that is supported by carefully researched evidence. The language that you use needs to be precise and uncluttered by unnecessary devices which have the potential to distract the reader, shift the meaning or detract from the clarity of the argument.

Padding

Padding consists of all the extra words added to writing that do not add anything to the meaning or content of the text. This includes:

1. Redundant phrases such as 'It is interesting/worthy/important to note that ...’ 'For what it's worth ...’.

2. Irrelevant material which has no bearing on your topic.

3. 'Dead' words includes words which repeat other words e.g. dead corpse, combined together.

4. Adverbs add quantity but little to the meaning. Adverbs such as: really, rather, quite, totally and so on, may not enhance your expression. For example: really obscure, rather tedious.

Making your writing sound formal

It is usually the case that in formal writing you avoid the use of personal pronouns. These include I, me, we, us and you. However there are instances in certain disciplines where your lecturer will invite you to use the first person. In reflective journal writing for example, using the first person is appropriate.
